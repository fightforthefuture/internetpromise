First, we sign people up to vote for the internet, and make a list of the internet candidates.

I stand for a free and open Internet.

I support transparent and participatory processes for making Internet policy and the establishment of five basic principles

  • Expression Don't censor the Internet.
  • Access Promote universal access to fast and affordable networks.
  • Openness Keep the Internet an open network where everyone is free to connect, communicate, write, read, watch, speak,
  • Innovation Protect the freedom to innovate and create without permission. Don't block new technologies, and don't punish innovators for their users' actions.
  • Privacy Protect privacy and defend everyone's ability to control how their data and devices are used.
